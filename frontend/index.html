What the data says about how the city moves.

Why this matters

Taxi demand isn't just a curiosity — it's a proxy for how a city actually moves. A 90% concentration of pickups in one borough is a fleet-allocation and curb-management problem for the TLC; a 23% overnight fare premium is a cost-of-mobility issue for shift workers who don't have the luxury of choosing when they travel; and the fact that 9 in 10 trips never leave their home borough challenges the assumption that taxis primarily serve cross-city commuting. Read together, these patterns are exactly the kind of signal congestion-pricing policy, transit investment, and equitable fare design need to account for.
